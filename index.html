Welcome to the homepage of Orthopaedic Engineering a collaboration within the Colleges of Science and Engineering and Medicine and Veterinary Medicine at the University of Edinburgh. The group carries out research in orthopaedic and biomedical engineering.



Particular research interests of the group are (click on the links to learn more):
        • Osteoporotic fracture fixation  
        • μFE analysis of bone
        • Strain-based plasticity models of bone
        • FE modelling of bone micro-cracking
        • Generation of virtual FE models of bone microstructure
        • Macro-models with muscular and ligamentous boundary conditions
        • Numerical and experimental evaluation of implants used in total knee arthroplasty
        • Constitutive modelling and experimental studies on bone graft
        • FE models of the acetabular construct following revision hip arthroplasty
        • Investigation of the stability of the tibial tray following knee arthroplasty
        • Investigation of fracture healing using 3D ultrasound 
        • Investigation of fracture during implant insertion
